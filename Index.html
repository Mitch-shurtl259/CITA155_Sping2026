What is March Madness

March Madness refers to the annual NCAA Division I basketball tournaments, one for men and one for women. Each tournament features 68 teams competing in a single‑elimination format—meaning one loss and you're out. Teams qualify by either winning their conference championship or being selected as an at‑large team by the NCAA selection committee. NCAA.org ESPN.com


When It Happens

  • Selection Sunday- When the full bracket is revealed
  • First Four- Play in games that reduce the feild from 68 to 64
  • First Round- Second Round- Sweet 16- Elite Eight- Final Four- Championship Game

Why its so popular:

  • Unpredictability: Lower‑seeded teams often upset favorites.
  • Brackets: Millions of fans fill out brackets predicting winners.
  • High drama: Close games, buzzer‑beaters, and emotional storylines.
  • National attention: It dominates sports media for three weeks every spring.
