How It Works?

March Madness is the NCAA’s huge single‑elimination college basketball tournament held every March, where 68 Division I teams compete for the national championship. It begins with Selection Sunday, when the full bracket is revealed and every team learns its seed and region. From there, the tournament moves fast — one loss and a team is out — creating the upsets, buzzer‑beaters, and unpredictable runs that make the event so famous. As the rounds progress from the First Four to the Final Four, the field shrinks until only two teams remain to play for the title. Millions of fans follow along by filling out brackets and watching the drama unfold across the month.
